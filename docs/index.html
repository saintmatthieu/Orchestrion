Your students play real music — rhythm first.

Orchestrion turns any score into an instrument. The student taps the rhythm — on a MIDI keyboard or the computer keyboard — and Orchestrion plays the notes of the piece, at the student's own pulse. Real repertoire, from day one.

How it works

1

Open a score

Start with the bundled pieces — from Frère Jacques to a Chopin nocturne — or open any MuseScore or MusicXML file.

2

Choose how to play

Plug in any MIDI keyboard, or use the computer keyboard as-is. There is nothing to configure.

3

Tap the rhythm

Each tap plays the next notes of the piece. The student owns the timing; Orchestrion takes care of the pitches.

For music teachers

  • Rhythm before note-reading. Students experience pulse, phrasing and musical flow with real repertoire, before pitch accuracy becomes a hurdle. Wrong-note anxiety is simply removed from the equation.
  • Your own repertoire. Orchestrion opens MuseScore (.mscz) and MusicXML (.mxl, .musicxml) files — use the material you already teach with.
  • Zero setup in the classroom. The computer keyboard works out of the box; a USB MIDI keyboard is detected automatically.
  • Free and open source. GPL-3.0 licensed, no account, no ads, works offline. Built on MuseScore's engraving and playback engine.
